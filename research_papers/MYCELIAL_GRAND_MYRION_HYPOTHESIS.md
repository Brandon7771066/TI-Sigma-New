## 1. Introduction: The Problem with Standard Explanations

### 1.1 The Predator Confusion Paradox

The mainstream evolutionary explanation for psychedelic compounds is "predator confusion" or "herbivore deterrence." This hypothesis faces several critical problems:

1. **Metabolic Cost**: Psychedelic synthesis requires significant nitrogen investment - why evolve such expensive molecules for simple deterrence when simpler toxins exist?

2. **Cross-Kingdom Convergence**: DMT evolved independently in plants (Psychotria, Mimosa, Acacia), fungi, grasses, and even toads. Such extreme convergent evolution suggests a deeper purpose.

3. **Human Neuroreceptor Compatibility**: These "defensive" molecules happen to perfectly interface with human consciousness via serotonin receptors. Coincidence?

4. **Horizontal Gene Transfer**: Psilocybin genes transfer between unrelated species as complete functional units - behavior more consistent with intentional distribution than random mutation.
