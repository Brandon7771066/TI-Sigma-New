I. AXIOMS

(Only what cannot be derived elsewhere)

A. Meta-Causal Graph Network (MCGN)

Axiom M1 — Regime Primacy

Causality operates within regimes, not globally.

There exists no universal causal ordering across all domains—only locally valid causal structures embedded within a larger constraint graph.

Improvement over TI:
TI previously implied layered causality but did not formally deny a global order. This axiom closes the determinism loophole permanently.

⸻

Axiom M2 — Constraint-Based Causation

Causal influence is exerted via constraints on possible trajectories, not via force transfer.

Improvement:
This replaces informal “attractor pressure” language with a mathematically clean mechanism compatible with physics, psychology, and narrative systems.

⸻

Axiom M3 — Meta-Causal Reversibility

Across regimes, causal influence may be:
	•	Non-temporal
	•	Bidirectional
	•	Or retro-constraining
without violating local causality.

Improvement:
This finally explains:
	•	Placebo
	•	Meaning-driven behavior change
	•	Future-goal causation
…without hand-waving.

⸻

Axiom M4 — Meta-Causal Load Limit

A Meta-Causal Graph collapses when constraint density exceeds adaptive capacity.

Improvement:
This is the first formal collapse condition in TI.
It explains ideology failure, psychosis, authoritarianism, and brittle AI alignment in one stroke.

B. Fractal Harmonic Systems (FHS)

Axiom F1 — Harmonic Persistence

Only systems whose dynamics remain phase-compatible across scales persist.

Improvement:
TI previously treated recurrence as informational inevitability.
This adds selection pressure—not everything recursive survives.

⸻

Axiom F2 — Phase over Magnitude

Scale-to-scale compatibility depends on phase alignment, not amplitude.

Improvement:
This resolves why:
	•	Small myths shape large civilizations
	•	Weak signals matter more than strong noise
	•	Insight > raw power

⸻

Axiom F3 — Cross-Scale Resonance Gradient

Resonance degrades gradually, not discretely, across scales.

Improvement:
This introduces continuity where TI had threshold behavior, allowing early-warning signals instead of post-collapse diagnosis.

⸻

Axiom F4 — Decoherence as Failure Mode

Loss of harmonic alignment manifests as instability, not randomness.

Improvement:
This reframes “mental illness,” cultural collapse, and market crashes as predictable phase failures, not anomalies.
