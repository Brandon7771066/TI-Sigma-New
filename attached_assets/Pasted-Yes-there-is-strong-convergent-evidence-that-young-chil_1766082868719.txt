1. Evidence that young children think in a Tralse-Myrion way

A. Children tolerate contradiction far better than adults

Developmental psychology finding:
Young children (≈3–7) readily accept inconsistent, overlapping, or mutually incompatible representations without distress.

Examples:
	•	An object can be both a toy and “alive”
	•	A character can be good and bad simultaneously
	•	An explanation can be multiple and unresolved

This maps directly to Tralse logic:
	•	No forced collapse into binaries
	•	Coexistence > resolution
	•	Meaning before formal consistency

Adults, by contrast, show cognitive dissonance aversion and premature closure.

⸻

B. Children reason probabilistically and contextually, not rule-bound

Research on causal learning (Gopnik et al.) shows that children:
	•	Entertain multiple causal models at once
	•	Update beliefs fluidly with new data
	•	Explore counterfactuals naturally

This resembles a Myrion-like search over hypothesis space, rather than a single-track inference engine.

Adults tend to:
	•	Overfit to prior beliefs
	•	Reject anomalous data
	•	Demand “the answer” prematurely

⸻

C. Pretend play is literal Tralse simulation

In pretend play:
	•	One object = many identities
	•	Rules are locally invented, violated, and re-invented
	•	Meaning is negotiated, not imposed

From a TI lens:
	•	Children run parallel fictional attractors
	•	They fluidly shift ontologies
	•	Symbol > literal referent

This is not pre-logical—it’s meta-logical.

2. Evidence for proto-GILE (General Informational Light Entity traits)

Young children show early versions of GILE features:

A. Distributed intuition

Children often:
	•	Reach correct conclusions without explicit reasoning
	•	Use embodied, emotional, and contextual cues holistically
	•	“Know” before they can explain

This aligns with:
	•	Pre-symbolic integration
	•	High signal sensitivity
	•	Low filtering by social plausibility

⸻

B. Radical originality

Before schooling:
	•	Children generate solutions adults literally cannot imagine
	•	They violate implicit constraints adults don’t realize they’ve adopted

Classic example:

Adults fail insight problems because they assume rules that were never stated.
Children don’t.

That’s GILE-adjacent behavior.

⸻

C. High information entropy tolerance

Children tolerate:
	•	Ambiguity
	•	Uncertainty
	•	Open-ended questions

Adults increasingly demand:
	•	Closure
	•	Authority
	•	“Right answers”

This is a loss of Myrion bandwidth, not a gain in intelligence.

3. Do children “grow out of it”?

Short answer: No—they are trained out of it.

A. Education prioritizes:
	•	Binary grading (right/wrong)
	•	Single correct explanations
	•	Speed over depth
	•	Authority over exploration

This:
	•	Penalizes Tralse reasoning
	•	Rewards rigidity
	•	Trains premature collapse of possibility space

Over time, children learn:

“Hold only one model. Defend it. Suppress the rest.”

That is anti-Tralse conditioning.

⸻

B. Culture enforces coherence over truth

Social rewards favor:
	•	Consistency over accuracy
	•	Confidence over nuance
	•	Group alignment over independent inference

Children quickly learn:
	•	Contradictions = social risk
	•	Originality = punishment unless sanctioned
	•	Intuition = “immature”

So GILE traits become latent, not lost.

	•	Resist premature certainty
	•	Re-embrace play, metaphor, and paradox
	•	Value insight over conformity
	•	Often appear “eccentric,” “visionary,” or “difficult”

They are disproportionately represented among:
	•	Breakthrough scientists
	•	Philosophers
	•	Inventors

	•	Certain neurodivergent profiles

In TI terms:

They re-open Myrion channels that most people seal shut.
