**Hypomanic temperament** is distinct from bipolar hypomania:

- **Impairment:** Enhancing vs sometimes impairing
- **Stability:** Stable baseline vs cycling
- **Control:** Volitional vs involuntary

**Characteristics:**
| Trait | Hypomanic Temperament | Clinical Hypomania |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Sleep need | 4-6 hours (stable) | Variable, decreasing |
| Energy | High but sustainable | Escalating |
| Productivity | Consistent high output | Erratic bursts |
| Social impact | Leadership | Sometimes grandiose |
| Mood | Predominantly positive | Elevated/irritable |
| Duration | Decades | Days to weeks |

## Section 6: Practical Implications

### 6.1 Can "Always On" Be Trained?

**Hypothesis:** Yes, through kundalini practices and coherence training.

**Training protocol:**
1. **HRV coherence training** (6 weeks)
   - HeartMath or similar
   - 20 minutes twice daily
   - Target: sustained 0.1 Hz rhythm

2. **Gamma meditation** (ongoing)
   - Loving-kindness or open awareness
   - Shown to increase gamma
   - 20 minutes daily

3. **Sleep optimization**
   - Focus on N3 efficiency
   - Consistent schedule
   - Reduce artificial light

4. **Passive kundalini practices**
   - As per separate protocol
   - May accelerate transition

**Expected timeline:** 3-6 months to measurable shift.

### 6.2 Risks and Cautions

**Not everyone should pursue "always on":**
- Bipolar vulnerability: May trigger episodes
- Anxiety disorders: May increase arousal unhelpfully
- Cardiac conditions: Consult physician
- Exhaustion: Must be genuine energy, not willpower

**Signs of healthy "on" state:**
- Sustainable over weeks/months
- No crash afterward
- Sleep remains restorative
- Relationships not impaired
- Judgment intact

**Signs of unhealthy activation:**
- Needing less and less sleep
- Escalating grandiosity
- Impaired judgment
- Relationship damage
- Crash following period
