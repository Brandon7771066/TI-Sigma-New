## Part 1: The Correlation→Causation Inference Method

### 1.1 Brandon's Key Insight

> "We can actually INFER causal effects from just the strengths of correlations!!!"

**Traditional view:** Correlation ≠ causation (always)

**Brandon's view:** High correlation strength can suggest causal direction when combined with:
1. Temporal sequence (cause precedes effect)
2. Plausible mechanism (affection → heart opening → love → kindness)
3. Dose-response relationship (more affection loss → worse outcomes)
4. Alternative explanation exclusion
5. Cross-domain consistency

### 1.2 The Method: Correlation Strength as Causal Evidence

**Granger Causality Principle:**
If variable A strongly predicts future values of variable B (controlling for B's past), AND the mechanism is plausible, AND timing fits, then A likely causally influences B.

**Application to affection decline:**

```
TIMELINE SEQUENCE:
1850-1890: High affection norms
    ↓
1890-1920: Homophobia rise (CAUSE)
    ↓
1920-1970: Affection withdrawal (MECHANISM)
    ↓
1970-2000: Isolation & loneliness surge (EFFECT)
    ↓
2000-2025: Peak meanness & mental health crisis (AMPLIFICATION)
```

If we find:
- ✅ Timing matches (affection loss precedes isolation)
- ✅ Strong correlation (r > 0.75)
- ✅ Dose-response (more loss → worse outcomes)
- ✅ Plausible mechanism (affection → heart opening → love)
- ✅ Cross-domain consistency (appears in multiple metrics)

Then correlation strength IS causal evidence.
