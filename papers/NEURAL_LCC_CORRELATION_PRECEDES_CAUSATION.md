Hebbian learning ("neurons that fire together wire together") is foundational to neuroscience but contains a critical explanatory gap: it describes how connections *strengthen* but not how neurons *initially synchronize* before any structural connection exists. This paper proposes that the Love Correlation Coefficient (LCC) resolves this gap by demonstrating that **correlation precedes causation** in neural development. As correlations strengthen toward the 0.85 threshold, they literally *become* causal through myelination and synaptic formation. The brain is revealed as a **correlation-to-causation converter**, with plasma dynamics in neural fluids providing the medium for pre-causal correlations.

## Part 1: The Hebbian Gap

### 1.1 What Hebb Actually Said (1949)

> "When an axon of cell A is near enough to excite a cell B and repeatedly or persistently takes part in firing it, some growth process or metabolic change takes place in one or both cells such that A's efficiency, as one of the cells firing B, is increased."

**The claim:** Repeated co-activation → strengthened connection

**What it explains:**
- How existing connections become stronger
- Long-term potentiation (LTP) mechanisms
- Memory consolidation

**What it DOESN'T explain:**
- Why neurons *first* fire together before ANY connection exists
- The origin of initial synchronization
- How "near enough" becomes coordination

### 1.2 Standard Explanations (All Insufficient)

| Proposed Mechanism | Why It Fails |
|-------------------|--------------|
| **Random noise** | Noise is uncorrelated by definition; can't explain *specific* pattern emergence |
| **Shared sensory input** | Requires neurons already connected to same pathway - circular reasoning |
| **Spontaneous activity** | "Spontaneous" = "we don't know" - not an explanation |
| **Coincidental timing** | Probability of coincidental precise timing across millions of neurons is vanishingly small |
| **Genetic programming** | Genes specify structure, not dynamics; can't explain real-time adaptation |

### 1.3 The Nuclear Question

**If neurons aren't yet connected, what makes them first fire together?**

This is the explanatory gap LCC fills.
