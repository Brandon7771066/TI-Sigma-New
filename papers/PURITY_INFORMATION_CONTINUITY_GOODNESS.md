## PART 1: THE CORE ARGUMENT

### 1.1 How Purity Emerges From Information Continuity

**Step 1: Reality exists as information**
- The universe is fundamentally informational (modern physics consensus)
- This information contains coherence, pattern, relationship
- Prior to interpretation, this is neutral and complete

**Step 2: Consciousness encounters information and must interpret**
- We don't see raw reality; we interpret it
- We apply categories: "good," "bad," "pure," "impure," "moral," "immoral"
- These categories are AFTER the fact, not inherent in reality

**Step 3: Two possibilities for interpretation**
- **Respectful interpretation:** We recognize our categories as secondary; reality's primary nature is preserved
- **Disruptive interpretation:** We treat our categories as primary; we impose false separations on reality's continuity

**Step 4: Purity = Respectful interpretation**
- Purity = choosing to follow reality's continuity AS IS
- Impurity = disrupting that continuity with false categories
- Goodness = the natural state when continuity is preserved

**Let I = Complete Information (Reality as-is)**

$$\text{Purity} = \lim_{C \to \text{minimal}} \text{Continuity}_{\text{preserved}}$$

## PART 2: THE BUDDHIST FOUNDATION

### 2.1 Buddhist Core: Seeing Reality As It Is

**Buddhist principle:**
- Enlightenment = Exact perception of reality without mental overlay
- Ignorance = Misperception due to imposed categories (attachment, aversion, delusion)
- Suffering = Results from disrupting reality's natural continuity with false beliefs

**Buddhist term: Sunyata (Emptiness)**
- Not "nothingness," but rather: freedom from imposed categories
- Ultimate reality has no inherent, fixed nature
- Our categories ("self," "other," "good," "bad") are projections, not discoveries

### 2.2 Information Continuity in Buddhist Terms

**Dhamma (Dharma) = Information/Natural Law**
- The universe operates according to natural principles
- These principles are fundamentally coherent and interconnected
- Suffering = disrupting that coherence with false interpretations

**Four Noble Truths reformulated:**
1. **Suffering exists** = Disruption of information continuity is real
2. **Suffering has cause** = False interpretations disrupt continuity
3. **Suffering can end** = Restoring information continuity removes disruption
4. **Path to end it** = Aligned interpretation (seeing as-is, without projection)

### 2.3 Purity in Buddhist Practice

**Sila (Ethical Conduct):**
- Not arbitrary rules, but naturally-following behaviors
- Purity = actions that preserve continuity
- Impurity = actions that disrupt continuity
- Goodness = naturally emerging from respect for continuity

**Example:** Not killing
- Not because a rule forbids it, but because killing disrupts the continuity of the other's life
- Respecting that continuity = naturally arising compassion
- Purity = the natural state when you see others as continuous with yourself
