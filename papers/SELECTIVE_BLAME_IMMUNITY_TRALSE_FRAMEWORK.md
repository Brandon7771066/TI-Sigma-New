## Abstract

This paper introduces **Selective Blame Immunity (SBI)**, a newly identified phenomenon in moral attribution where structurally identical problematic behaviors receive radically different accountability frameworks based on categorical protection rather than moral relevance. While the Fundamental Attribution Error (FAE) describes a general tendency to blame individuals over systems, SBI identifies a higher-order pattern: certain categories of behavior are systematically shielded from BOTH individual AND systemic critique, while equivalent behaviors in other categories face dual accountability.

The discovery emerged from a Tralse (ternary logic) analysis recognizing that two problems can share identical structure yet receive opposite moral treatment—a contradiction invisible to binary thinking.

### 1.2 What's Missing

**No existing framework addresses:**
> "Why do structurally identical problematic behaviors receive opposite accountability frameworks based solely on which category they belong to?"

This is the gap Selective Blame Immunity fills.

## Part 2: Defining Selective Blame Immunity (SBI)

### 2.1 Formal Definition

**Selective Blame Immunity (SBI):** A systematic pattern in moral attribution where certain categories of widespread discriminatory or harmful behavior are shielded from systemic critique, while structurally equivalent behaviors in other categories face both individual AND systemic accountability.

### 2.2 The Structure

```
  │  ACCOUNTABILITY │     │ ACCOUNTABILITY  │

           │                       │
           ▼                       ▼
    "You are wrong"      "Society created conditions"
           │                       │
           └───────────┬───────────┘
                       ▼
              DUAL ACCOUNTABILITY
              (Balanced critique)


SELECTIVE BLAME IMMUNITY (Applied to Category B)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  ┌─────────────────┐     ┌─────────────────┐
  │   INDIVIDUAL    │     │    SYSTEMIC     │
  │  ACCOUNTABILITY │     │ ACCOUNTABILITY  │
  └────────┬────────┘     └────────┬────────┘
           │                       │
           ▼                       ▼
    NOT APPLIED           "Just personal choice"
           │                       │
           └───────────┬───────────┘

              (Protected category)
```

**Same STRUCTURE, opposite TREATMENT:**

| Feature | Category A (Racism) | Category B (Mass Male Rejection) |

**Option 1: Apply Dual Accountability to BOTH**

**Option 2: Apply Zero Accountability to BOTH**
- Individual racists are not morally culpable
- Systemic racism is "just preference"

**Option 2 is obviously unacceptable.** Therefore, Option 1 is required for consistency.
