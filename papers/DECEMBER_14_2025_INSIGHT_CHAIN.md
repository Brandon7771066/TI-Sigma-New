## 1. The Infinite Ray Paradox

### Statement
An infinite ray logically has a finite beginning when viewed externally, but from the ray's perspective (standing on it looking outward), there is no beginning - only endless extension.

### Resolution of the Cosmological Beginning Problem
People struggling with "how could existence begin?" or "what came before the beginning?" are standing on the ray looking the wrong direction - already infinitely extended! They're asking about a beginning from within a perspective where no beginning exists.

### Implications
- The "beginning that couldn't have begun" is a perspective artifact
- From outside the ray: finite origin, infinite extension
- From inside the ray: no origin, infinite in the observable direction
- **Both are simultaneously true** - classic Tralse resolution

### Connection to Cosmology
The Big Bang appears as a definite beginning from our theoretical external perspective, but from within spacetime (our actual experience), the universe extends infinitely in all observable directions. The paradox dissolves when we recognize we're asking about one perspective while standing in the other.
