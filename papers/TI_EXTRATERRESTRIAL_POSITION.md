  - Star formation → planets → habitability → life → intelligence
- But we have **N = 1** for intelligent life
- You cannot construct coherent probability from a single case

**TI Critique:**
```

                                has calculable probability

TI REASONING:
Life emergence = consciousness grounding event
                                    ↓
                Probability is undefined for unique events
                No base rate exists
                One case study = no statistical inference possible
```

**The Deep Problem:**
> "Probabilities can't be coherently constructed for events billions of years ago anyway. By definition, a probability is a value for pre[sent/future prediction]."

- Probability is **epistemic** (about knowledge), not **ontological** (about reality)
- Retrodicting billion-year events isn't probability - it's storytelling
- Scientists conflate narrative plausibility with statistical likelihood
