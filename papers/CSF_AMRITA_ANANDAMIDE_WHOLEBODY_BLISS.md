## Abstract

**Background:** Ancient yogic traditions describe "Amrita" (nectar of immortality) as a divine secretion flowing through the central channel, producing states of bliss and transcendence. Modern neuroscience identifies cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) and the endocannabinoid anandamide ("bliss molecule") as potential substrates for these experiences.

**Hypothesis:** CSF serves as the physiological Amrita, carrying bliss-promoting neurotransmitters throughout the CNS, while anandamide provides whole-body bliss via both central and peripheral nervous system receptors.

**Integration:** The FAAH protocol must target BOTH systems: (1) CSF-mediated CNS bliss via enhanced anandamide, serotonin, and melatonin, and (2) PNS-mediated whole-body bliss via peripheral CB1/CB2 receptors.

**Clinical Implications:** Complete suffering mitigation requires dual CNS + PNS intervention, explaining why localized treatments often fail while holistic approaches (yoga, meditation, FAAH inhibition) succeed.
