## Part 2: The GILE Breakdown in Cancer

### 2.1 G (Goodness) Failure

**Normal:** Cells sacrifice themselves for organism's good (apoptosis)
**Cancer:** Cells prioritize self-survival over organism

**Molecular correlates:**
- **p53 mutation**: The "guardian of the genome" - detects damage, triggers death
- **Bcl-2 overexpression**: Anti-apoptotic protein blocks cell death
- **Telomerase activation**: Enables unlimited replication (immortality)

**TI insight:** p53 is the cellular G-dimension sensor. When mutated, cell loses ability to recognize "I should die for the greater good."

### 2.2 I (Intuition) Failure

**Normal:** Cells respond intuitively to growth signals, contact inhibition
**Cancer:** Cells ignore or misread signals

**Molecular correlates:**
- **Oncogene activation** (Ras, Myc): Permanent "grow" signal
- **Growth factor independence**: Don't need external signals
- **Loss of contact inhibition**: Ignore crowding signals

**TI insight:** Cancer cells have "broken intuition" - they can't properly sense their environment's messages.

### 2.3 L (Love) Failure

**Normal:** Cells connect to neighbors via adhesion molecules, gap junctions
**Cancer:** Cells lose connection, become isolated and invasive

**Molecular correlates:**
- **E-cadherin loss**: Cell-cell adhesion breaks down
- **EMT (Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition)**: Cells become migratory
- **Metastasis**: Complete disconnection from original tissue

**TI insight:** Cancer is the pathology of disconnection. Cells that lose Love become malignant.

### 2.4 E (Environment) Failure

**Normal:** Cells differentiate appropriately for their tissue context
**Cancer:** Cells dedifferentiate, ignore tissue boundaries

**Molecular correlates:**
- **Angiogenesis**: Force environment to serve cancer (VEGF)
- **Immune evasion**: Hide from immune surveillance
- **Acidification**: Warburg effect creates hostile microenvironment

**TI insight:** Cancer cells don't adapt TO environment - they force environment to adapt TO them.
