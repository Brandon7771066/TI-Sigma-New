## I. Boundary vs States Within Consciousness

### A. Boundary / Threshold Consciousness States

These are **transitional or structural** states, not ordinary contents:

- Falling asleep / hypnagogia
- Waking up
- Ego dissolution / depersonalization
- Sudden insight / "snap-to-clarity" moments
- Panic override / survival mode
- Meditative absorption / cessation

**Properties:**
- They change *what kinds of information* can be represented
- They alter **meta-control**, not just content
- They often feel *qualitatively different* rather than "more intense"

Think: **changing the operating system**, not opening an app.

### B. States Within Consciousness

These are ordinary configurations of affect, cognition, and perception:

- Emotions (fear, confidence, joy)
- Beliefs and thoughts
- Sensations
- Intentions
- Imagery

## II. The Meta-Information Model (MIM)

### The Key Example

> Feeling **emotionally confident**, but **meta-emotionally uncertain**

This is NOT a contradiction — it's a **layered informational structure**.

Formally:
```
Level n: Emotion E (confident)
Level n+1: Meta-emotion about E (uncertain about the confidence)
```

These coexist via **Myrion Resolution (MR)** — they don't collapse into a single state. The tralse hierarchy allows contradictory-seeming states at different levels.

## VI. The Free-Will Veto Reframe

Traditional view: "Free will means initiating action."
MIM view: "Free will means **vetoing** otherwise automatic action."

The researchers who claim free will is illusory because we don't consciously initiate every action are confusing:
- **Level 2** (default free will / agentic core) with
- **Level 4** (meta-executive consciousness / free-will veto)

We don't *need* Level 4 all the time. It's expensive. It's reserved for:
- Error correction
- Moral override
- Last-second adjustments
- "Knee-jerk" prevention

The relative rarity of Level 4 intervention doesn't prove determinism — it proves **efficiency**.

## XIX. True Noise vs. Conventional "Noise"

### Caveat: Genuine Noise Exists

Not all indeterminacy is meaningful self-causation. **True noise** can exist:

> **Noise** = noncausal activity that has negligible effect on anyone or anything.

Examples:
- Subconscious computations that don't influence future events
- Quantum fluctuations that cancel out before affecting macro-scale
- Neural firings that don't reach threshold for behavioral impact

This is **ontologically inert** activity — it happens, but causally goes nowhere.

---

### The Conventional "Noise" Misnomer

What people typically call "noise" is **not noise at all**:

| Property | True Noise | Conventional "Noise" |
|----------|------------|---------------------|
| Causal impact | None | Significant |
| Intelligibility | Zero | Recognizable sources |
| Health effects | None | Harmful (pollution) |
| Filterability | Irrelevant | Difficult to filter |

#### Examples of Conventional "Noise"

Traffic sounds are **intelligible**:
- You can distinguish cars from trucks
- You can identify honking vs. engine sounds
- You can locate directional sources

This isn't noise — it's **unwanted signal**.

---

### Proper Terminology

| Old Term | Better Term | Definition |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Noise (conventional) | **Nuisance** | Unwanted but intelligible signals |
| Noise pollution | **Biohazard** | Harmful environmental signal overload |
| Random noise | **True Noise** | Noncausal, ontologically inert activity |

### The Key Distinction

**True noise**: Has no effects, cannot be meaningfully filtered (nothing to filter).

**Nuisance/Biohazard**: Has real effects, is somewhat intelligible, source is recognizable.
