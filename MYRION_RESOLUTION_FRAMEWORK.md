## The Myrion Resolution Framework

### Core Concept

**Structure:**
```
"It is [x] [Attribute A] and [y] [Attribute B] but ultimately [z] [Resolution]"
```

**Scale:** (-3, 2) for x, y, z
- **-3:** Strong negation ("extremely NOT this")
- **-2:** Moderate negation
- **-1:** Weak negation
- **0:** Indeterminate / balanced / neither
- **+1:** Weak affirmation
- **+2:** Strong affirmation ("very much this")

**Extension:** For values outside (-3, 2), use **natural logarithm**
- Below -3: ln(|value|) with negative sign
- Above +2: ln(value)

### Key Innovation: The "Ultimately" Statement

**The Resolution (z value) is NOT an average!**

It represents the **emergent truth** that arises from synergizing the contradictions.

**Example:**
- "It is +1 Quantum and +1 Classical but ultimately **+2 Classical**"
- Interpretation: Both quantum and classical aspects present equally, BUT the emergent phenomenon is **strongly classical** (measurable, reproducible, macroscopic)
